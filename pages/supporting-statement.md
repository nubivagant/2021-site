## Supporting statment

### Associate Director of Data and Digital Transformation, Teenage Cancer Trust

Hello hiring team!

I've spent the last eight years working as a transformation design consultant at the intersection of people, technology and change, leading work with organisations in the public and third sectors to help them become more capable, better organised, and more genuinely useful to the people they serve. The Associate Director role at Teenage Cancer Trust sits firmly in that space, and presents as an opportunity to apply all of this experience purposefully for the next decade of my career in a leadership role.

**Leading transformation in mission-driven organisations**
Right now, I'm providing interim product leadership for a large charity client, working within their frontline delivery function as it transitions from siloed service lines into a new model built around the full service lifecycle. My role spans operating model design, product function development, team structure, and capability building, while keeping BAU delivery on track across three new products in active development.

This is precisely the challenge set out in the candidate pack that Teenage Cancer Trust faces: a DDaT function that needs to shift its operating model, strengthen capability, and move towards more iterative, outcome-focused ways of working while continuing to deliver. What I've learned from this and other complex transformation programmes is that in mission-driven organisations, the human systems and the technical systems are equally consequential. Getting the culture, the trust, and the ways of working right is what makes technology actually land.

**Building DDaT functions from the ground up**
Before moving into consulting, I worked in Local Government establishing the Council's first dedicated digital services team - a new group of DDaT professionals spanning design, technology, data and product. That experience taught me what it takes to build a function that earns trust across a wider organisation: clarity of purpose, visible value through early delivery, and a consistent approach to bringing people on the journey rather than doing transformation to them.

Across my time at TPXimpact, I've continued to shape DDaT capability in different contexts – designing digital maturity assessment frameworks, building professional development pathways, and leading operating model redesign for DDaT functions in complex organisations. In a large and complex central government department for example, I developed a portfolio prioritisation and demand management approach that improved senior leadership decision-making across their technology and product estate. I'm comfortable designing the governance and structures that make a function work, not just the vision for what it could become.

**User-centred design and product thinking as a way of working**
I believe product thinking isn't a methodology, it's a mindset shift. In my work we tend to refer to this as internet era ways of thinking. The organisations I've worked with that have made the most meaningful progress are those where iterative, evidence-led approaches have become part of how people think, not just something a team in one corner of the organisation does. I've helped embed this in organisations with low digital maturity and sceptical senior stakeholders, and I've learned to read the room to know when to advocate clearly for user-centred approaches and when to demonstrate their value quietly through delivery.

At another public sector organisation, combining service design, technology delivery and organisational change to extend adoption and fostering capacity meant holding together a technical build and a people change programme simultaneously, whilst keeping focus on what it meant for the families the service was designed to reach. That grounding in outcomes, not just outputs, is something I'd bring directly to this role.

**Influencing at senior level and building across the organisation**
Much of my work has required building trusted relationships quickly with senior leaders who may be sceptical, time-pressured, or navigating significant political complexity. I've learned to translate complex technical and organisational concepts into language that resonates with people whose primary frame is policy, finance, or service delivery. Recently I was responsible for an operating model that required close partnership with civil service leadership, elected members, and specialists from Arup and other disciplines - a genuinely multi-disciplinary stakeholder environment where influence had to be earned rather than assumed.

I believe that's the kind of horizontal influence this role requires. Shaping DDaT strategy as part of the SLT, building data literacy and confidence across the charity, and connecting technology potential to outcomes that matter to young people with cancer, and the skilled colleagues that deliver these services internally.

**Why this role, why now**?
I'm drawn to this opportunity because it brings together the things I care most about: using digital and data as genuine force multipliers and enablers of impact, building the kind of team that can sustain that work, and doing so inside an organisation with a clear and important mission. I'm also at a point in my career where stepping into a permanent leadership role rather than working across multiple client engagements is the right next step. I've developed deep practice and craft expertise and want to be somewhere long enough to see change take root.

Teenage Cancer Trust is working with young people at one of the most difficult moments of their lives. That context shapes everything. For how services need to be designed, how data needs to be handled, how the digital experience needs to feel. I'd be proud to help build the capability that makes that work better.

Thank you for your consideration, and I'm more than happy to discuss any aspect of my experience in more detail and look forward to the conversation.

Best of luck with the recruitment,

Kurtis Johnson
