## Cover note

Dear hiring team!

I'm drawn to this role as it sits at an intersection I've been working across for over eight years: organisational change, capability, and the messy realities of transformation. All within contexts where the stakes genuinely matter.

At TPX Impact I've led complex consulting engagements across government, health and housing, working with senior leaders and multidisciplinary teams on challenges that span culture, operating model design, and capability development. Almost all of that work has required holding the space between strategic intent and delivery reality. I've owned facilitating difficult conversations, building trust with client stakeholders, and translating complex organisational problems into coherent programmes of change.

My interest in Arup is longstanding. After following the work of former Arup employees like Dan Hill, and more recently through my work on the New Towns programme with MHCLG. I had the opportunity to work alongside Arup colleagues, notably Jack Stevens in the Cities, Planning and Design function. Recent conversations with Jack reinforced something I've admired in the quality of thinking at Arup, and the depth and seriousness with which you approach the human needs of large-scale built environment projects. The idea of bringing people and organisational change expertise to that kind of infrastructure context is really compelling to me.

I bring experience across the technical areas you describe: operating model and organisation development, leadership & capability, and culture change. I'm comfortable with ambiguity, experienced in leading teams of consultants, and have contributed to business development and proposal work throughout my career. I'd welcome the chance to explore whether my background is the right fit for what you're building in this consulting practice.

Many thanks
Kurtis
